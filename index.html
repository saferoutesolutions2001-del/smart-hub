Privacy Policy — JRM SmartRoute

Effective date: 10 July 2026  |  App: JRM SmartRoute  |  Package: com.saferoutesolutions.jrm_smartroute  |  Publisher: SafeRoute Solutions

SafeRoute Solutions ("we", "us", "our") operates the JRM SmartRoute mobile application (the "App"), used by drivers to view company-published routes, hazard alerts, authorized rest stops, and route weather guidance. This policy explains what information the App collects from you, how it is used, and the choices you have.

The App does not require you to create an account, sign in, or provide a name, username, password, or email address. There is no in-app authentication of any kind. Access is set up at the depot/company level: when a logistics depot becomes our customer, we make that depot's routes and safety data available on our servers. A driver using the App simply enters the 4-digit Depot ID given to them by their depot — there is no personal login step, and the App does not verify who the individual driver is.

1. Information We Collect From You

CategoryWhat we collectWhy
Depot & vehicle identifiers The 4-digit Depot ID you enter, and the vehicle number you enter To fetch the routes, hazards, and rest-stop data published for your depot, and to identify a vehicle in an SOS alert. Neither is a personal account — they are not paired with a name, email, or password
Location information Precise (GPS) device location, used on-device for the map and hazard-proximity alerts. Location is sent to our servers only at the moment you press the in-app SOS button To show your position on the route map and alert you to nearby hazards and authorized rest stops (all on-device), and — only if you trigger SOS — to send your location, vehicle number, depot ID, current destination, and an optional name you may type in, so your depot can respond to the emergency
Camera Live camera feed, used only when you open the in-app QR scanner To scan a QR code for quick destination lookup. The camera feed is decoded on-device only; no image or video is ever stored, saved to your gallery, or transmitted to our servers
App preferences (stored on your device only) Depot ID, vehicle number, selected destination, language preference, and whether hazard voice alerts are enabled To remember your settings between sessions. These stay on your device; only Depot ID and vehicle number are also sent to our servers, where needed, to fetch or report data for your depot

We do not collect your name, email address, username, password, contacts, SMS/call logs, photos from your gallery, microphone audio, or any data unrelated to the functions described above. The App contains no advertising SDKs and no third-party analytics or crash-tracking SDKs.

2. How We Use Information

  • To fetch and display the route, hazards, and rest-stop data published for your depot
  • To display your live position, route, and nearby hazards on the map (processed on-device)
  • To alert you (visually and by voice) when you approach a known hazard or authorized rest stop
  • To relay an SOS alert — including your location, vehicle number, depot ID, destination, and optional name — to your depot if you trigger it
  • To provide customer support if you contact us about an issue

3. How We Share Information

We do not sell your information. We share it only in these limited circumstances:

  • Your depot — an SOS alert (location, vehicle number, destination, optional name) is relayed by email to the admins registered for the Depot ID in use, so they can respond to the emergency. To build the map image in that email, your SOS location is sent to Mapbox to generate a static map snapshot.
  • OpenStreetMap — the visual map background you see while browsing the route is loaded directly by your device from OpenStreetMap's public map servers. This exposes your device's IP address and the approximate map area you are viewing (not your precise GPS location) to OpenStreetMap. If OpenStreetMap is unavailable, the App requests the map background through our backend instead.
  • Law enforcement or government authorities — only in response to a valid court order, subpoena, or other legally binding request, or where necessary to investigate a safety incident (e.g. an SOS alert).

4. Data Retention

  • SOS alerts — not stored on our servers at all. An SOS alert is sent once as an email to your depot's registered admins; we keep no database record of it.
  • Server access logs — our servers keep standard request logs (which may include your Depot ID and device IP address) for up to 15 days for security and troubleshooting purposes, then they are deleted. We may shorten this further in the future.
  • Locally stored app preferences — remain on your device until you clear the App's data or uninstall it, which removes them immediately.

5. Data Security

All data in transit between the App and our servers is encrypted (HTTPS/TLS).

6. Children's Privacy

The App is intended for use by commercial vehicle drivers and is not directed at, marketed to, or knowingly used by children. We do not knowingly collect information from anyone under the age of 18.

7. Your Choices & Rights

  • You can enable or disable location and camera access at any time through your device's system settings; note that disabling location will prevent map, navigation, hazard-alert, and SOS features from working.
  • Because the App has no personal account, stored data is either local to your device (cleared by uninstalling) or a short-lived server log (see Section 4). You or your depot administrator may contact us at the email below with any question about data we hold.
  • You can stop all data collection by uninstalling the App.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the App or applicable law. The "Effective date" above will be updated accordingly. Continued use of the App after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
